Intro

As a computer science student at Tennessee Technological University, I am challenged everyday by my professors and peers to grow my knowledge in the field. I started at TTU in the fall of 2022 after attending the University of North Alabama (Fall 2021-Spring 2022). I made the choice to transfer, because I knew that I specifically wanted to go into cybersecurity, and the was only offered as a minor at UNA. I felt as if this was the best move for me to make so that I could pursue my passions at a higher level.

I am now in my third year of university, but have academic standing as a senior. Throughout high school and even throughout college, I have set high standards for myself that has allowed me to get to this point in my education. I was enrolled in dual enrollment and AP courses in high school, and I also enrolled in summer classes after I transferred to get caught back up where I had to retake classes that did not transfer. In my time at TTU, I have been involved in the ACM and ACM-W organizations, as well as more recently becoming involved in the offensive and defensive cyber interest groups. I also serve in the postion of teaching assistant for the College of Engineering here at TTU, where I assist in an introductory programming course.
